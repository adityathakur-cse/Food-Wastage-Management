Guidance to effectively manage and reduce food wastage in India :

Prevention:

  • Plan your meals ahead of time. This will help you avoid overbuying and cooking too much food
  • Store food properly. Make sure to refrigerate or freeze food promptly after purchase.
  • Check your refrigerator and pantry regularly to use up food before it expires
  • Cook only what you need. Avoid overcooking leftovers.
  • Don't be afraid to eat imperfect produce. Many fruits and vegetables are still perfectly edible even if they are bruised or have blemishes.

    • Reuse:

  • Use leftover cooked food in salads, soups, or stir-fries.
  • Turn stale bread into croutons, bread pudding, or stuffing.
  • Dry out leftover fruits and vegetables for snacks or trail mix.
  • Use vegetable peels and scraps to make stock or compost.

    • Recycle:

  • Compost food scraps and yard waste. This creates nutrient-rich soil that can be used to grow more food.
  • Donate unwanted food to food banks or soup kitchens.
  • Use commercial food waste recycling services.

    • Government and industry:

  • The government should implement policies to reduce food waste at all stages of the supply chain, from farm to fork. This could include measures such as subsidies for composting, food donation programs, and stricter food safety standards.
  • Food businesses should adopt practices to reduce food waste, such as better inventory management, improved packaging, and reduced portion sizes.

    • Awareness and education:

  • It is important to raise awareness of the problem of food waste and its impact on the environment and society. This can be done through public campaigns, school programs, and social media.
  • People need to be educated about how to prevent food waste, reuse food scraps, and recycle food waste. This can be done through public education campaigns, online resources, and community workshops.
